# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

**Positive Behaviors**
- ✅ **Be respectful**: Treat others with dignity and respect
- ✅ **Be inclusive**: Welcome newcomers and diverse perspectives
- ✅ **Be collaborative**: Work together constructively
- ✅ **Be helpful**: Support others in learning and growing
- ✅ **Be patient**: Understand that everyone has different experience levels
- ✅ **Be constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and suggestions
- ✅ **Be professional**: Maintain professional communication standards

**Community Guidelines**
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accept constructive criticism
- Focus on what is best for the community
- Show empathy towards other community members
- Help others learn and grow

### Unacceptable Behavior

**Prohibited Actions**
- ❌ **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, bullying, or intimidation
- ❌ **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language or actions
- ❌ **Personal Attacks**: Insults, ad hominem attacks, or personal criticism
- ❌ **Spam**: Unwanted commercial content or self-promotion
- ❌ **Trolling**: Deliberate provocation or disruptive behavior
- ❌ **Doxxing**: Sharing private information without consent
- ❌ **Threats**: Any form of threat or intimidation
- ❌ **Offensive Content**: Sexually explicit, violent, or hateful content

**Specific Examples**
- Using sexualized language or imagery
- Making unwelcome sexual advances
- Publishing others' private information
- Making threats of violence or harm
- Creating a hostile or intimidating environment
- Deliberately spreading misinformation
- Engaging in personal vendettas

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders
**Project Maintainers**
- Enforce this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently
- Respond to reported incidents promptly and appropriately
- Take appropriate corrective measures
- Document enforcement actions for transparency

**Enforcement Guidelines**
- **Warning**: For minor violations, issue a private warning
- **Temporary Suspension**: For repeated or serious violations
- **Permanent Ban**: For severe violations or repeated offenses
- **Escalation**: Involve platform moderators when necessary

### Reporting Process

**How to Report**
1. **Direct Contact**: Email project maintainers privately
2. **GitHub Issues**: Create a private issue with "Code of Conduct" label
3. **Community Managers**: Contact designated community managers
4. **Anonymous Reporting**: Use anonymous reporting channels when available

**What to Include**
- Description of the incident
- Date and time of occurrence
- Parties involved
- Any relevant screenshots or evidence
- Desired resolution (if any)

**Response Timeline**
- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Initial Review**: Within 48 hours
- **Resolution**: Within 7 days (complex cases may take longer)

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

**Online Spaces**
- GitHub repositories and issues

- Discussions and forums
- Social media channels
- Email communications

**Offline Spaces**
- Meetups and conferences
- Workshops and events

**Project Activities**
- Code contributions
- Documentation work
- Community support
- Testing and feedback
- Mentoring and guidance

## Conflict Resolution

### Disagreement Resolution

**Constructive Disagreement**
- Focus on issues, not people
- Provide evidence and reasoning
- Listen to opposing viewpoints
- Seek common ground
- Escalate to maintainers if needed

**When Disagreements Arise**
1. **Direct Communication**: Try to resolve directly first
2. **Mediation**: Ask a maintainer to mediate
3. **Formal Process**: Use formal reporting if necessary
4. **Final Decision**: Accept maintainers' final decision

### Appeal Process

**Appealing Enforcement Actions**
- Submit appeal to project leadership
- Provide additional context or evidence
- Review within 5 business days
- Final decision is binding

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Standards

**Professional Communication**
- Use clear, concise language
- Avoid jargon when possible
- Provide context for technical discussions
- Assume good intent from others

**Inclusive Language**
- Use gender-neutral language
- Avoid cultural stereotypes
- Be mindful of time zones and language barriers
- Respect different communication styles

### Contribution Guidelines

**Code Contributions**
- Follow project coding standards
- Provide clear commit messages
- Include tests when appropriate
- Document changes thoroughly

**Community Participation**
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Share knowledge and experience
- Provide constructive feedback
- Celebrate community achievements

## Consequences

### Enforcement Actions

**Warning**
- Private message explaining the violation
- Request to correct behavior
- Documentation of the warning
- Follow-up monitoring

**Temporary Suspension**
- Suspension from community spaces (1-30 days)
- Removal of access to project resources
- Required review of Code of Conduct
- Conditions for reinstatement

**Permanent Ban**
- Permanent removal from all community spaces
- Blocking from project repositories
- Reporting to platform administrators
- No possibility of reinstatement

### Rehabilitation

**Reinstatement Process**
- Acknowledgment of Code of Conduct
- Understanding of specific violations
- Commitment to future compliance
- Probationary period (if applicable)
